## 📖 Tutorial & Tips

Welcome to the touch typing guide! This section compiles the most essential tips and tricks to help you build speed and accuracy from the ground up.

---

### Posture & Ergonomics

Proper posture is crucial for preventing fatigue and injury (RSI — Repetitive Strain Injury).

| Rule | Recommendation |
|------|----------------|
| 🪑 **Sit Straight** | Keep your back straight and supported by your chair. |
| 💪 **Elbow Position** | Keep elbows bent at approximately **90 degrees**. |
| 🖥️ **Screen Distance** | Maintain at least **45–70 cm** between your eyes and screen. |
| 🤲 **Wrist Position** | Wrists may lightly touch the tabletop, but **never rest your body weight on them**. |
| 👣 **Feet** | Keep both feet flat on the floor. |

---

### Home Row Position

The **Home Row** is the central row of keys where your fingers always return after pressing any other key.

```
LEFT HAND          RIGHT HAND
  A  S  D  F   |   J  K  L  ;
  👆 💍 🖕 👉   |   👈 🖕 💍 👆
Little Ring Mid Idx | Idx Mid Ring Little
```

- **Left Hand:** `A`, `S`, `D`, `F`
- **Right Hand:** `J`, `K`, `L`, `;`
- **Tactile Bumps:** The `F` and `J` keys usually have small raised bumps to help you find the home row without looking.
- **Thumbs:** Rest lightly above the **Space bar**.

---

### Finger Scheme

Each finger is responsible for a specific set of keys. This "zone" approach ensures maximum efficiency and minimum hand movement.

```
  Q  W  E  R  T  |  Y  U  I  O  P
  A  S  D  F  G  |  H  J  K  L  ;
  Z  X  C  V  B  |  N  M  ,  .  /
```

| Finger | Keys Covered |
|--------|-------------|
| Left Pinky | `Q`, `A`, `Z`, `Shift`, `Caps Lock`, `Tab` |
| Left Ring | `W`, `S`, `X` |
| Left Middle | `E`, `D`, `C` |
| Left Index | `R`, `F`, `V`, `T`, `G`, `B` |
| **Thumbs** | **Space Bar** |
| Right Index | `Y`, `H`, `N`, `U`, `J`, `M` |
| Right Middle | `I`, `K`, `,` |
| Right Ring | `O`, `L`, `.` |
| Right Pinky | `P`, `;`, `/`, `Enter`, `Backspace`, `'`, `Shift` |

> 💡 **Tip:** Always press `Shift` with the **opposite hand** from the letter you are capitalizing.

### Technique Tips

- 🚫 **Don't Look at the Keys** — This is the golden rule. Looking at your hands destroys muscle memory.
- 🎯 **Accuracy First, Speed Second** — Speed is a natural byproduct of accuracy. Typing fast with errors means practicing mistakes.
- 🎵 **Find Your Rhythm** — Try to maintain a steady, even rhythm. Keystrokes should come at equal intervals.
- 🤏 **Minimize Movement** — Keep hands close to the home row. Use finger reach instead of moving the whole hand.
- 🔁 **Return to Home Row** — Always return fingers to home row position after pressing any key.
- ❌ **One Key at a Time** — Press and release cleanly. Avoid holding keys down.

---

### Practice Routine

| Duration | Frequency | Focus |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| 15–30 min | Daily | Core practice sessions |
| 5–10 min | Before starting work | Warm-up exercises |
| ∞ | Always | Apply touch typing in real work |

> 🔑 **Consistency beats intensity.** 20 minutes every day is far more effective than 3 hours once a week.

## ⌨️ Keyboard Layouts

Most resources support multiple keyboard layouts. Here's a quick overview:

| Layout | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **QWERTY** | The most common keyboard layout worldwide; default for most resources here. |
| **Dvorak** | Designed in 1936 for efficiency; vowels on the left hand, common consonants on the right. |
| **Colemak** | Modern alternative to QWERTY; similar layout but optimized for reduced finger movement. |
| **Workman** | Designed to reduce lateral finger movement compared to Colemak. |
| **AZERTY** | Standard layout used in French-speaking countries. |
| **QWERTZ** | Standard layout used in German-speaking countries. |

> 🛠️ Tools like [**keybr.com**](https://www.keybr.com), [**The Typing Cat**](https://thetypingcat.com), and [**Klavaro**](https://klavaro.sourceforge.io) support multiple layouts.
